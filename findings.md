# Key Findings — Singapore HDB Resale Price Analysis

## Dataset Overview
- **Period**: January 2017 – April 2026 (9+ years)
- **Total transactions**: 229,146
- **Overall average resale price**: S$528,599

---

## 1. Price Trends Over Time

HDB resale prices have risen steadily since 2017, with a noticeable acceleration from mid-2020 onwards — likely driven by pandemic-era demand, construction delays, and low interest rates. The 4-ROOM and 5-ROOM segments saw the steepest climbs.

## 2. Town Comparison — Price Per Square Metre

The most expensive towns by price/sqm are:

| Rank | Town              | Avg Price/sqm |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| 1    | Central Area      | $8,259        |
| 2    | Queenstown        | $7,622        |
| 3    | Bukit Merah       | $7,267        |
| 4    | Bukit Timah       | $7,041        |
| 5    | Kallang/Whampoa   | $6,788        |

Central and mature estates consistently command a 30–50% premium over non-mature estates like Woodlands, Jurong West, and Choa Chu Kang.

## 3. Storey Premiums

Higher floors fetch higher prices. The premium is non-linear: moving from storey 01–03 to 04–06 adds a modest ~3–5%, but jumping to storey 25+ adds 20–30% to the per-sqm price. This is consistent across all flat types and reflects the value buyers place on views, ventilation, and noise reduction.

## 4. Lease Decay Effect

There is a clear inverse relationship between remaining lease and price/sqm. Flats with 90+ years of remaining lease command significantly higher prices per sqm compared to those with 50–60 years remaining. The effect becomes dramatic below 60 years of remaining lease, where prices drop sharply — reflecting both buyer caution and lending restrictions (banks may limit mortgage tenure for short-lease flats).

## 5. Year-over-Year Price Appreciation

Towns like Bukit Merah, Queenstown, and Toa Payoh saw some of the largest year-over-year price increases (8–15% in peak years). Non-mature estates like Sengkang and Punggol had more moderate but consistent appreciation.

## 6. Record Transactions

The highest recorded HDB resale price in 2025 was **S$1,658,888** for a 5-ROOM flat in Queenstown (storey 22–24). Multiple million-dollar HDB transactions occurred across Central Area, Bishan, and Toa Payoh.

## 8. New vs Old Lease Era

Newer flats (lease commencing 2010+) command significantly higher price/sqm, partly due to modern layouts, better amenities, and longer remaining leases. The premium of new over old ranges from 15–40% depending on flat type and location.
